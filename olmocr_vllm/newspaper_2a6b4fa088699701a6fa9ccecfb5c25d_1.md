After a review of the best available scientific and commercial information, we find that listing the Huachuca-Canelope population of the Arizona treefrog, the Arkansas darter, black mudtail, Highlands tiger beetle, Dichanthemum (=panicum) hieritii (Hirst Brothers’ panic grass), two Kentucky cave beetles (Louisville cave beetle and Tatum Cave beetle), relict leopard frog, sicklefin redhorse sucker, and Stephen’s rifle beetle is not warranted at this time. However, we ask the public to submit to us at any time any new information that becomes available concerning the stressors to any of the 10 species listed above or their habitats.\n\nDATES: The findings announced in this document were made on October 6, 2016.\n\nADDRESSES: Detailed descriptions of the basis for each of these findings are available on the Internet at http://www.regulations.gov at the following docket numbers:\n\n| Species | Docket No. |\n|---------|------------|\n| Arizona treefrog (Huachuca-Canelope population) | FWS-R2-ES-2016-0111 |\n| Arkansas darter | FWS-R6-ES-2016-0113 |\n| Black mudtail | FWS-R5-ES-2016-0114 |\n| Highlands tiger beetle | FWS-R4-ES-2016-0105 |\n| Dichanthemum (=panicum) hieritii (Hirst Brothers’ panic grass) | FWS-R4-ES-2016-0115 |\n| Kentucky cave beetles (Louisville cave beetle and Tatum Cave beetle) | FWS-R4-ES-2016-0116 |\n| Relict leopard frog | FWS-R4-ES-2016-0117 |\n| Sicklefin redhorse sucker | FWS-R4-ES-2016-0118 |\n| Stephen’s rifle beetle | FWS-R4-ES-2016-0119 |"}
