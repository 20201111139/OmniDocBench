Synthesis and electro-optical properties of conjugated ionic polymers

Tai-Long Gui, Sung-Ho Jin, Jong-Wook Park, Kwon-Taek Lim, Sang-Youl Kim, Yeong-Soon Gal

Abstract

A new ionic conjugated polymer, poly(2-ethyl-1-N-methylpyridinium iodide) (PEMPI), was synthesized by the direct polymerization of 2-ethylpyridine and methyl iodide under mild reaction condition. Another similar conjugated polymer with different counter-ion, poly(2-ethyl-1-N-methylpyridinium tetrachloroborate) (PEMTPB) was also prepared by the ion-exchange reaction of poly(2-ethyl-1-N-methylpyridinium iodide) using sodium tetrachloroborate. The polymer structures were characterized by various instrumental methods to have conjugated polymer system with pyridyl moieties of different counter-ion. The photoluminescence spectra of these polymers having different counter-anions indicated that there is no different inductive and size effects of counter-anion between iodide and tetrachloroborate anions in this conjugated polymer backbone.

Keywords: Polyacetylene; 2-Ethylpyridine; Ionic polymer; Ion-exchange reaction; Luminescence

1. Introduction

Polyacetylene and its homologues have been studied as organic semiconductors [1–3], membranes for gas separation and for liquid-mixture separation [4], chiro-optical materials [5,6], side-chain liquid crystals [7–9], materials for chemical sensors [10], and materials for nonlinear optical property [11,12] and photoluminescence and electroluminescence properties [13–17].

Polyacetylene with ionic nature have potential as materials for mixed ionic and electronic conductivities, energy storage devices such as batteries and permselective membranes, and light-emitting devices due to their extensive conjugation and various functionalities [2,18]. The synthesis of simple mono- and disubstituted ionic polyacetylenes from ethynylpyridines had been carried out with the activation of the acetylene bond in ethynylpyridines [18,19]. We have also studied on the synthesis and characterization of the poly(2-ethyl-1-N-phenyl)borne conjugated polymers having various functionalities [2,9,14].

In this paper, we report the synthesis of novel ionic conjugated polymers having different counter-anion (iodide, tetrachloroborate) and the characterization of resulting ionic conjugated polymers.

2. Experimental

The conjugated ionic polymer, poly(2-ethyl-1-N-methylpyridinium iodide) (PEMPI) was prepared by the direct polymerization of 2-ethylpyridine with methyl iodide without any initiator system in methanol solvent. The polymerization was performed at 70 °C after stirring for 1 h because of the low boiling temperature of methyl iodide. Another ionic polymer, poly(2-ethyl-1-N-methylpyridinium tetrachloroborate) (PEMTPB) was easily prepared by the ion-exchange reaction of PEMPI with sodium tetrachloroborate in methanol solvent.
