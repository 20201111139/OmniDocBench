To avoid irritation of added \(\mathrm{NH}_{4}{ }^{+}\)in the soils and the effects of the associated counter-ions (e.g., \(\mathrm{SO}_{4}{ }^{2-}, \mathrm{Cl}^{-1}\) ), we used urea-N together with a nitrification inhibitor, dicyanadiamide, which can build up high levels of \(\mathrm{NH}_{4}{ }^{+}-\mathrm{N}\) accumulation with little \(\mathrm{NO}_{3}{ }^{-}-\mathrm{N}\). This allowed us to compare the inhibition by \(\mathrm{NH}_{4}{ }^{+}-\mathrm{N}\) with that by \(\mathrm{NO}_{3}{ }^{-}-\mathrm{N}\) of the soil \(\mathrm{CH}_{4}\) uptake. We also studied the effects of dicyanadiamide addition alone on the \(\mathrm{CH}_{4}\) oxidation in surface forest soils.
\section*{Materials and methods}
Soil sampling and chemical analysis
After removal of the above-ground plant debris soil samples were taken at Numata farm \(\left(36^{\circ} 36^{\prime} \mathrm{N}, 139^{\circ} 00^{\prime} \mathrm{E}\right.\), altitude \(780-810 \mathrm{~m}\) ), Chiba University, from a typical Japanese cedar forest (Cryptomeria japonica, 40 years old) and its adjacent orchard dominated by apple trees (Malus pumila, 15 years old) in November 2002. The typical forest land lies \(200 \mathrm{~m}\) above the orchard. The surface slopes of both sites are within the range of \(7-12^{\circ}\). Three different soil layers, based on the soils' attributes, and the size of \(100 \pm 0.26\) to 56 to 51 , and 51 to \(75 \mathrm{~cm}\) depth under the conumous forest, and from 0 to 9, 9 to 23 and 23 to \(51 \mathrm{~cm}\) under the soil moisture (100000000000000000000 , 51\()\) soils, classified as anisdol (FAO soil classification), were sieved \((2 \mathrm{~mm})\) to remove small stones and roots, where laboratory and stored in the dark below \(5^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\) prior to incubation. \(\mathrm{NH}_{4}{ }^{+}-\mathrm{N}\) and \(\mathrm{NO}_{3}{ }^{-}-\mathrm{N}\) in the soils were extracted with \(1 \mathrm{M} \mathrm{KCl}\) solution. The slurry was shaken for \(30 \mathrm{~min}\) before filtering. The \(\mathrm{NH}_{4}{ }^{+}-\mathrm{N}\) and NO \({ }_{3}-\mathrm{N}\) in the soil extract was measured colorimetrically via the nitroprusside and hydrazine-reduction methods, respectively (Kim 1995). Soil water content was measured by drying the soil at \(105^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\) for \(24 \mathrm{~h}\).
