\section*{Conclusion}
Hexacondicate dichroisolane/pyridine adducts have been synthesised in various aprotic solvents by the direct reaction and \(\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{SiCl}_{2}\) with pyridine. The first example of an \(\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{SiCl}_{2}-2 \mathrm{Rpy}\) compound bearing non-coplanar pyridine rings has been found, although a coplanar arrangement of the pyridine ligands seems to be the most typical conformation in the solid state. Quantum chemical analysis demonstrates that both coordination modes may be favourable. Some of these pyridine adducts (those bearing 4-methylphenyl) are not known to undergo ligand distribution when extracted with a two-dimensional to yield the hexacondate siliconium salts \(\left[\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{Si}(\mathrm{Ry}) \mathrm{P}\right]_{4}\), the ion \(\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{Si}\) of which is stabilized by chloroform molecules.
The \({ }^{29} \mathrm{Si}^{\text {CP/MAS NMR spectra of the adducts }}\) \(\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{SiCl}_{2}-2 \mathrm { Rpy}\) reveal large spans for the shielding tensors of their octahedally coordinated silicon nuclei, and GIAO calculated have provided an insight into the directions of the \(\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{SiCl}_{2}\) and \(\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{SiCl}_{2}\) and \([\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{Si}-\mathrm{H}]\). The electrons should be determined by the tensors, which point almost along the \(\mathrm{Cl}-\mathrm{Si}-\mathrm{Cl}, \mathrm{H}-\mathrm{Si}-\mathrm{H}\) and \(\mathrm{N}-\mathrm{Si}-\mathrm{N}\) axes, respectively. The highest shielding is observed in the direction of the \(\mathrm{Si}-\mathrm{H}\) and \(\mathrm{Si}-\mathrm{N}\) bonds, and the shielding is considerably lower in the direction of the \(\mathrm{Si}-\mathrm{Cl}\) bonds. Quantitative descriptions of those shielding tensors, however, are difficult due to general problems with the modelling of \(\mathrm{H}-\mathrm{Si}\) bond influences on \({ }^{29} \mathrm{Si}\) NMR properties, which are not known.
Four possible valence structures \(\mathbf{A}\) - \(\mathbf{D}\) have been investigated with the NBO method, with the valence structures \(\mathbf{B}\), \(\mathbf{C}\) and \(\mathbf{D}\) being equally good descriptions of the bonding situation. The NBO analysis reveals that these structures contain polar covalent \(\mathrm{Si}-\mathrm{H}\) bonds, strong polar \(\mathrm{Si}-\mathrm{Cl}\) bonds, and that the highest bond polarisation is observed for the \(\mathrm{Si}-\mathrm{N}\) interaction, which therefore has to be considered as a donor-acceptor interaction. Analysis of the topological properties of the electron density distribution (AIM) suggests that the Lewis structure \(\mathbf{C}\) is the best description for the bonding situation in molecules of the type \(\mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{SiCl}_{2}-2 \mathrm Rpy}\).
