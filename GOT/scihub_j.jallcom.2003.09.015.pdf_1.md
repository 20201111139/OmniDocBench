\({ }^{\mathrm{b}}\) Data of our investigation.
The formation of solid solution regions for the binary compounds was not revealed, and practically no variation of the lattice parameters of the ternary bidders in two- and three-phase samples was observed (Table 2). This fact indicates that no homogeneity ranges exist for the ternary compounds.
The ternary compounds \(\mathrm{Ln}_{2} \mathrm{NiB}_{10}\) where \(\mathrm{Ln}-\mathrm{Y}, \mathrm{Gd}, \mathrm{Tb}\), Dy, Ho with the \(\mathrm{Th}_{2} \mathrm{NiB}_{10}\) structure type were reported in . The prepared the sample of the composition \(\mathrm{Er}_{2} \mathrm{NiB}_{10}\) to verify the formation of the bider while of the same structure in the \(\mathrm{Er}-\mathrm{Ni}-\mathrm{B}\) system. This can indicate the existence of polymorphism for this bider. Taking into account that the bider which of the \(\mathrm{ErNiB}_{4}\) type structure was found by a single-crystal method, and that the bider while of the \(\mathrm{YCrB}_{4}\) type structure was found by X-ray powder diffraction, we assumed the high-temperature modification to have the \(\mathrm{ErNiB}_{4}\) structure and the low-temperature modification to have the \(\mathrm{YCrB}_{4}\) partition structure. The results of a DTA investigation revealed a transition temperature of \(1540 \mathrm{~K}\).
A new compound of the approximate composition \(\mathrm{ErNiB}_{2}\) with unknown structure was found. The X-ray powder pattern of this compound differed from the powder patterns of the known ternary bidders found in the earlier studied \(\mathrm{Ln}-\mathrm{Ni}-\mathrm{B}\) systems. This compound exists only in the as-cast sample and decomposes into the \(\mathrm{Er}_{2} \mathrm{Ni}_{2} \mathrm{~B} 6\) and \(\mathrm{ErNiB}_{2}\) compounds after annealing. Since the single crystals were not available its structure was not determined.
A further new compound was found in the as-cast samples containing 16.6 at. \(\%\) Er. The X-ray powder pattern of this compound was very similar to the powder pattern of the \(\mathrm{ErNi}_{4} \mathrm{~B}\) boreide. The crystal structure of the new \(\mathrm{Er}_{0.917} \mathrm{Ni}_{4.0} 9 \mathrm{~B}\) boreide was determined using an X-ray single-crystal method: space group \(P 6 / \mathrm{mmm}\); \(a=1.48399 \mathrm{~nm} ; c=0.69194 \mathrm{~nm}\) . This structure is very similar to the \(\mathrm{Y}_{0.915} \mathrm{Ni}_{4.12} \mathrm{~B}\) structure . The \(\mathrm{Er}_{0.917} \mathrm{Ni}_{4.95} \mathrm{~B}\) boreide decomposes after annealing, and the main phase is the \(\mathrm{ErNi}_{4} \mathrm{~B}\) compound.
