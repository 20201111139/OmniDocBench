The condition of the distribution of the four opportunists may be associated with physical or biological conditions on the glacier surface. The alga of Raphidomena has been found in eastern Europe and in the Antarctic, and one of the species of Raphidomena (Koliella) was reported as a shade-adapted species (Komárek et al., 1973). However, the distribution of Raphidomena on this glacier cannot be explained by the shade-light characteristics. The distribution of this alga (mainly at S4 and a little at S1) corresponded to the area where higher species diversity occurred relative to the other sites. The alga may prefer an area with high species diversity and may be able to survive in areas with keen species competition. The distribution of Oscillatoriacean 1 may be associated with the distribution of cryoconite holes. A cryocenter hole is a water-filled depression on the glacial ice surface, and is inhabited by many organisms, along the algae, cyanobacteria, rotifers, and crass.
