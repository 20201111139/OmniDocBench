## Assessment of the uncertainty

The results of the analyses of the uncertainty of the field data and related assumptions are shown in Figs 13 and 14.

Polygons np, nM, and nD in the x-y plane represent the spatial distribution of any possible xady, that was obtained from the data sets measured at sites P, M, and D, respectively. The degree of overlap between polygons is expressed as PPM (for overlap between np and nM) and PMD (for overlap between nM and nD) (see Eq. (11)).

There is no overlap between np and nM (i.e. PPM = 0) when y; ≤ 0.35 km and y; ≥ 1.05 km. With y; = 0.40-1.00 km, on the other hand, np and nM overlap each other (i.e. 0 < PPM < 1). However, the highest value of PPM (at )i = 0.40 km) is as low as 0.137. Excluding the cases that yi=0.40-0.45 km, the overlap is negligible (PPM ~ 0). On the other hand, the overlap of MM and nD is in a significantly different trend from that between np and nM. There is some overlap between nM and nD (0 < PMD) when yi = 0.20-0.90 km. At any magnitude of yi within this range, PMD is much larger than that of PPM (Fig. 14). The highest values of PMD (0.55-0.60) are seen when yi = 0.50 -0.70 km. With yi=0.45 ± 0.10 km, PMD ranges between 0.31 and 0.55, still much larger than the highest of PPM.
