For consistency, the time derivative of the constraints of (10) must vanish and hence they must have vanishing Poisson bracket with H. Using the fundamental Poisson brackets

$$\left[U(x),\Pi^{U}(y)\right]=\delta(x-y),\tag{13}$$

etc., we find that the primary constraints of (10) imply the secondary constraints

$$(\Sigma,\Sigma_{i})=\left(-\partial_{k}\Pi_{k}^{N},\varepsilon^{ijk}\partial_{j}\left(\Pi_{k}^{B}-mV_{k}\right)-\mu^{2}\,B_{i}\right).\tag{14}$$

If u2 = 0 (the Cremmer-Scherk model Lagrangian [1]), the constraints of (14) would become reducible as then 0; 2; = 0 and only the transverse portions of 2; are constraints. Furthermore, with u2 + 0, the requirement 2i = 0 leads to a tertiary constraint

$$T_{k}=\mu^{2}\Pi_{k}^{B}=0\tag{15}$$

with Di and Tk constituting second class constraints as

$$\left[T_{k}(x),\,\Sigma_{i}(y)\right]=\mu^{4}\delta_{ik}\delta(x-y).\tag{16}$$

All other constraints are first class and no further constraints need to be imposed for consistency. There are consequently five first class constraints (Φ', ΦA and Σ) and six second class constraints (Σ; and Tk). The constraints Φ and Σ correspond to the usual gauge transformations δWo = 002, 8W; = 0,2 associated with a gauge field Wn, while @ is associated with the fact that in (12) Ak acts merely as a Lagrange multiplier (i.e., it is not dynamical) and hence its value is completely arbitrary. Suitable gauge conditions associated with the first class constraints are

$\left(\gamma^{U},\gamma_{k}^{A},\gamma^{V}\right)=\left(U,A_{k},\partial_{k}V_{k}\right)=0$.  
  

From (10), (14), (15) and (17) it is evident that the only dynamical degrees of freedom are

$$V_{i}^{T}=\left(\delta_{ij}-\partial_{i}\partial_{j}/\partial^{2}\right)V_{j}.\tag{18}$$

We can verify this directly by explicitly eliminating the non-physical degrees of freedom in (4). First, one decomposes Vk, Ak and Bk into transverse (T) and longitudinal (L) parts where

$\nabla\times\mathbf{V}^{L}=0=\nabla\cdot\mathbf{V}^{T}$, (19)

etc., (4) now becomes

$$2L=\left(\mathbf{\hat{B}}^{L}\right)^{2}-\left(\nabla\cdot\mathbf{B}^{L}\right)^{2}+\left[\mathbf{\hat{B}}^{T}-\nabla\times\mathbf{A}^{T}\right]^{2}+\left(\mathbf{\hat{V}}^{T}\right)^{2}-\left(\nabla\times\mathbf{V}^{T}\right)^{2}+\left[\mathbf{\hat{V}}^{L}-\nabla U\right]^{2}$$
 
$$\quad+2m\left[\mathbf{V}^{T}\cdot\left(\nabla\times\mathbf{A}^{T}\right)+\mathbf{B}^{L}\cdot\mathbf{\hat{V}}^{L}+\mathbf{B}^{T}\cdot\mathbf{\hat{V}}^{T}-\mathbf{B}^{L}\cdot\nabla U\right]+2\mu^{2}\left[\mathbf{A}^{T}\cdot\mathbf{B}^{T}+\mathbf{A}^{L}\cdot\mathbf{B}^{L}\right].\tag{20}$$

The equations of motion for A - and U, respectively, imply that

$${\bf B}^{L}=0=\dot{\bf V}^{L}-\nabla U,\tag{21}$$

reducing (20) to

$$2L=\left(\dot{\mathbf{V}}^{T}\right)^{2}-\left(\mathbf{\nabla}\times\mathbf{V}^{T}\right)^{2}+\left[\dot{\mathbf{B}}^{T}-\mathbf{\nabla}\times\mathbf{A}^{T}\right]^{2}+2m\mathbf{V}^{T}\cdot\left(\mathbf{\nabla}\times\mathbf{A}^{T}\right)+2m\mathbf{B}^{T}\cdot\dot{\mathbf{V}}^{T}+2\mu^{2}\mathbf{A}^{T}\cdot\mathbf{B}^{T}.\tag{22}$$

Since

$${\bf A}^{T}\cdot{\bf B}^{T}=-\big{(}\nabla\times{\bf A}^{T}\big{)}\cdot\big{(}\nabla^{2}\big{)}^{-1}\big{(}\nabla\times{\bf B}^{T}\big{)},\tag{23}$$

we can eliminate V × AT from (22) to obtain

$$\nabla\times{\bf A}^{T}=\hat{\bf B}^{T}-m{\bf V}^{T}+\mu^{2}\big{(}\nabla^{2}\big{)}^{-1}\big{(}\nabla\times{\bf B}^{T}\big{)}.\tag{24}$$
