Catalysts employed for hydrocracking of F-T waxes are similar to those conventionally used for hydrocracking of petroleum-derived feeds, that is, they are of bifunctional nature, comprising a metal component dispersed on an acidic porous support. Since the F-T products are almost free of sulfur; the preferred metal phase is a noble metal, such as Pt and Pd, while amorphous oxides such as SiO2-Al2O3, and zeolites (Y, beta, mordenite) are commonly used as acidic carriers. When hydroisomerization instead of hydrocracking is the main target of the long-chain n-alkane hydroconversion step, as it occurs in isodewaxing processes, catalysts based on unidirectional 10-member ring zeolites (ZSM-22, ZSM-23, ZSM-35, ZSM-48) or silicoaluminophosphate molecular sieves (SAPO-11, SAPO-31, SAPO-41) as acidic supports have been proposed. A review dealing with suitable catalysts for the hydroisomerization of long-chain n-paraffins has been recently published by Deldari.

On a well-balanced catalyst, the hydrocracking activity and selectivity mainly depends on the acid properties of the support. Thus, catalysts based on amorphous silica-alumina having moderate Bronsted acidity are more selective towards middle distillates but are less active than those based on zeolites. In the case of zeolitic catalysts, improved selectivity towards middle distillates can be attained by reducing the density of Bronsted acid sites, that is, by increasing the Si/Al ratio, or by decreasing their acid strength through partial or total isomorphous substitution of framework Al3+ by other trivalent cations. However, the selectivity obtained after reducing the zeolite acidity is always accompanied by a decrease in the catalyst activity, thus requiring higher reaction temperatures to achieve a given degree of conversion.

Therefore, there is still great interest in developing new catalysts for the hydrocracking-hydroisomerization of high-boiling n-paraffins (waxes), such as those produced in the F-T process, which exhibit high activity at reaction temperatures as low as possible while keeping good selectivity towards the desired hydrocracked fraction i.e. middle distillates. In this sense, it has been recently reported that Pt/WOx-ZrO2 catalyst (0.5 wt.% Pt; 6.5 wt.% W) prepared by impregnation and calcined at 700 °C displays high activity and good selectivity towards branched isomers in the diesel and lube-base oil range during the hydrocracking of Fischer-Tropsch waxes. Unfortunately, while the behaviour of Pt/WOx-ZrO2 catalysts for the hydroisomerization of short-chain n-alkanes (C4-C2) is well documented in the literature, the amount of work dealing with heavier n-alkanes that would be more representative of the waxy Fischer-Tropsch products is very scarce.

# 2. Experimental

# 2.1 Preparation of catalysts

A series of tungsten-doped zirconia samples (abbreviated as WZ) with tungsten loading in the range of 6-22 wt.% W have been prepared by coprecipitation in the presence of polyvinyl alcohol (PVA) following the procedure described in Ref: [53] with some modifications. Chiefly, 300 cm3 of a 0.53 M zirconyl chloride (Aldrich; 98% ZrOCl2·8H2O) and ammonium metatungstate ((NH4)6H2W12O40, Fluka, 285% WO3) aqueous solutions were heated at 100 °C in an oil bath and kept at this temperature for 1 h. Besides, 300 cm3 of a polyvinyl alcohol (PVA, MW: 7-106 X 104, Sigma) aqueous solution (100 g/1) were heated at 100 °C in an oil bath for 1 h. Then, the two solutions were mixed and kept at 100 °C for an additional hour. After that, the mixed solution was cooled until 70 °C, slowly added to a NH4OH (2 M) buffer solution to keep the pH constant at 10, and kept for one additional hour in static. The formed transparent gel filaments were extensively washed with distilled water until the absence of chlorides and dried at 100 °C overnight and at 200 °C for an additional 12 h period.
