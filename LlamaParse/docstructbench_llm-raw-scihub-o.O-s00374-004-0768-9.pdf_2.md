To avoid nitrification of added NH4+ in the soils and the effects of the associated counter-ions (e.g., SO4, Cl-1), we used urea-N together with nitrification inhibitor; dicyandiamide, which can build up high levels of NH4+-N accumulation with little NO2-N. This allowed us to compare the inhibition by NH4+-N with that by NO2-N of the soil CH4 uptake. We also studied the effects of dicyandiamide addition alone on the CH4 oxidation in surface forest soils.

# Materials and Methods

# Soil Sampling and Chemical Analysis

After removal of above-ground plant debris, soil samples were taken at Numata farm (36°36'N, 139°00E, altitude 780-810 m), Chiba University, from typical Japanese cedar forest (Cryptomeria japonica, 40 years old) and its adjacent orchard dominated by apple trees (Malus pumila, 15 years old) in November 2002. The typical forest land lies 200 m above the orchard. The surface slopes of both sites are within the range of 7-12%. Three different soil layers, based on the soils' attributes, were sampled from 0 to 26, 26 to 51, and 51 to 75 cm depth under the coniferous forest; and from 0 to 9, 9 to 23, and 23 to 51 cm under the orchard, respectively. Fresh soils, classified as andisol (FAO soil classification), were sieved (2 mm) to remove small stones and roots in the laboratory and stored in the dark below 5°C prior to incubation. NH4-N and NO2-N in the soils were extracted with M KCl solution. The slurry was shaken for 30 min before filtering. The NH4+-N and NO2-N in the soil extract was measured colorimetrically via the nitroprusside and hydrazine-reduction methods, respectively (Kim 1995). Soil water content was measured by drying the soil at 105°C for 24 h. The total soil C and N contents were measured using CN analyzer (MT-700).
