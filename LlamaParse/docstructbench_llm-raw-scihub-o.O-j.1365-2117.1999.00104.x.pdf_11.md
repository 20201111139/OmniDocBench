# Interpretation

Tir 2 has not been drilled in the Tuscan Shelf; but it has similar character and stratigraphic position to Seq 2. Therefore; it probably correlates with Lit 2 + Lit 3 of the Volterra Basin. This correlation is characterized by well-defined, discontinuous, closely spaced reflections, normally onlapping onto the basal unconformity B*_ Toward the top, the reflections become more widely spaced and less defined. At the bottom, in several profiles, the reflections are mounded and very well defined (T7 sp. 400-500, Fig; 19; T8 sp. 1000-1100, Fig: 20).

Tir 2 is interpreted to be late Tortonian to early Messinian in age. If this is correct, it implies that in the Tuscan Shelf the equivalent of Seq (Lit 1) has not been recognized and therefore we have preferred to use Tir 0 instead of Tir 1, possibly equivalent of Seq 1 recognized only in the Ombrone area (lines: T44, TSB, T6, T7, T8; Figs 13, 16-20).

In places, Tir 3 is characterized either by prograding clinoforms (line TSB, sp. 2400, Fig: 17 and line T8, sp. 600-500, Fig: 20) or transparent pattern.
