As shown in Figure 6a, the Cul and CulA are double bridged by two different thiocyanate anions while the Cul and CulB (or CulC) are bridged by only one, and we define them as double-bridging and single-bridging modes with the Cu-Cu distances of 5.01 and 5.78 A, respectively. Furthermore, twelve Cu' atoms are linked together by eighteen thiocyanate anions to form 48-membered macrocyclic ring which stacks along the axis with distance of 14.9980(22) A between two adjacent rings and result in pipeline motif as shown in Figure 6b. Each 48-membered macrocycle is lateral-shared using its double-bridging sides with the circumjacent six macrocycles from different pipelines which also serve as the border of the central pipe by using their single-bridging sides to make it closed one.

Thus, the combination of the staggered arrangement of pipelines and the lateral-sharing mode among the different pipes make novel 3D porous framework of complex 5 with large cavities of 13.1848(16) A in diameters (Figure 6b). The another feature in complex 5 is the protonation of the TPBA-2 ligands occurred along with the reduction of Cu"l. Two protonated TPBA-2 ligands stack in pairs occupying the cavity.

Selectively desorption and sorption of THF by complex 1: As discussed above complex 1 has large capsule-like cavity with encapsulation of THF molecules. Further investigations on the desorption and sorption properties of complex were carried out: The TGA data of complex 1 showed that the loss of the uncoordinated solvents occurred from 60 to 190°C and the desolvated species is stable up to about 250°C. The results of X-ray powder diffraction (XPRD) analysis (Figure 7) and solid-state 1C NMR spectral measurements (Figure 8) showed that complex can selectively desorb and adsorb THF molecules. The XPRD pattern shows that complex 1 lost its crystallinity (Figure Tc) after being heated at 150°C for 24 h, probably due to the collapse of the layer structure. However, the crystallinity can be recovered upon immersing the desolvated species in THF for one day and as indicated by the XPRD pattern shown in Figure 7d. While in the most cases the reconstruction of the crystallinity occurs with more or less modification, the complete reestablishment of crystallinity is rarely reported [15].
