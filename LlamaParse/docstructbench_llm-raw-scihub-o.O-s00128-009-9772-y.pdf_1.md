# Determination of Pesticide Residues in Honey Samples

Irani Mukherjee

Received: 41 July 2008 Accepted: 5 May 2009 Published online: 23 June 2009

Springer Science+Business Media; LLC 2009

# Abstract

Protocol for the determination of pesticides residues in honey samples have been standardized using simple technique of liquid-liquid extraction. The method is sensitive to detect low levels of pesticides in honey. Honey sample was fortified with pesticides, namely, cypermethrin, fenvalerate, alphamethrin, lamba-cyhalothrin, endosulfan (1, and sulfate) and chlorpyrifos. The method of extraction and clean up was optimized and validated in the laboratory. The method was applied to screen six samples of honey locally available for pesticides residues. Recoveries ranged from 60% to 90.69% with RSDs from 29 to 10%. Low recoveries were recorded for % and 8 -endosulfan in the range of 609-71%. The LOQs varied from 0.05 to 1.0 mg kg. The presence of pesticide residues in honey has impelled the need for setting up monitoring programs to determine the proper assessment of human exposure to pesticides making possible to take policy decisions in the interest of health hazard.

# Keywords

Honey, Pesticides, residue analysis, GLC

Pesticides play a beneficial role in agriculture, because they help to combat the variety of pest that destroy crops, even though small amounts of pesticide residues remain in the food supply, constituting potential risk for human toxicity. The most widely used pesticides are organophosphorus and carbamates, which have almost completely replaced organochlorine pesticides. The extensive distribution of these groups of pesticides causes bees that have been fed on contaminated blossom to transfer pesticide residues into honey and finally the consumer. Different national regulations have established maximum concentrations of pesticide residues (MRLs) permitted in honey. As an example, Germany, Italy and Switzerland have set MRLs (Rissatoa et al. 2007) for amitraz, bromopropylate, coumaphos, cyamizole, flumetrine, and fluvalinate, which oscillate between 0.01 and 0.5 mg kg in Germany, between 5 and 500 mg kg in Switzerland and are of 10 mg kg in Italy. Up to now, maximum limits of pesticide residues in honey are not included in the Codex Alimentarius (1998). The European Union (EU 1996, 1999) legislation has regulated the MRLs for three acaricides: amitraz, coumaphos and cyamizole, which are 0.2, 0.1, and mg kg respectively (EC 1990).
