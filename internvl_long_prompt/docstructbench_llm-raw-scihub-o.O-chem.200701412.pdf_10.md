# Octahedral Pyridine Adducts of Dichlorosilane

## Conclusion

Hexacoordinate dichlorosilane/pyridine adducts have been synthesised in various aprotic solvents by the direct reaction of H3SiCl2Rpy with pyridine. The first example of an H3SiCl2Rpy adduct bearing a non-coordinating pyridine ring has been found, although a coplanar arrangement of the pyridine solid state seems to be the most typical analytical demonstration of coordination modes that both coordination modes may be favourable.

Some of these pyridine adducts (those bearing 4-methylpyridine) have shown to be extracted with hot chloroform to yield the hexacoordinate silicon salts [H3SiCl2Rpy]Cl2, the anion of which is stabilised by the formation of hydrogen bonds (H···Si) between the Si-Cl bonds and the Cl- anions. The 29Si CP/MAS NMR spectra of the adducts H3SiCl2Rpy reveal large spans for the shielding nuclei, and TGA calculations have provided an insight into the directions of the principal components (11), (22) and (33) of the shielding tensor, which points almost along the Si-H and N-Si-N axes, respectively. The highest shielding is observed in the direction of the Si-H and Si-N bonds, and the Si-Cl bonds. Quantitative descriptions of those shielding tensors, however, are difficult due to general problems with the modelling of 29Si NMR properties, which have not yet been overcome.

Four possible valence structures A-D have been investigated with the valence method, with A-D being equally good descriptions of the bonding situation. The NBO analysis reveals that these structures B and C are equally good descriptions of the bonding situation. The NBO analysis reveals that these structures B and C correspond to tan polar Si-Cl bonds, and that the highest bond polarisation is observed for the Si-N interaction, which therefore has to be considered as a donor-acceptor interaction. Analysis of the topological properties of the electron density distribution (AIM) suggests that the Lewis structure C is the best description for the bonding situation in molecules of the type H3SiCl2Rpy.

The quantum chemical calculations were carried out using the GAUSSIAN 03 program suite.[20] The solid-state geometries obtained from X-ray structure analysis were used as the starting geometries for the calculations. The calculations were performed at the density functional theory (DFT) level using Becke's three-parameter hybrid exchange functional[21] with the 6-311+G(2d,p) basis set for all atoms. The 6-311+G(2d,p) basis set was performed at the B3LYP/6-311+G(2d,p) level with the geometries obtained from the X-ray structure analyses.

The AIM analysis was performed with the AIM2000 program.[22] The electron density topology was analysed by using the Gaussian 03[23] program. The NBO analyses were performed with NBO 3.0.[24] The different valence structures A-D were generated with the "CHOOSE" option in the NBO program.

Relaxed potential energy surface (PES) scans were performed with the option "Opt=ModRedun" in Gaussian 03[23] with this option includes the specification of redundant internal coordinates. In these cases the torsion angle was optimised in every step whilst restricting the geometry of the molecule was completely optimised in every step whilst restricting access to a defined section of the potential energy surface.

The principal components of the NMR shielding tensors were extracted from the spectra using the HB-MAS program (D. Fenzke, Leipzig 1999).

NMR shielding tensors were calculated by the Gauge-Independent Atomic Orbital method (GIAO)[25] obtained at the B3LYP/6-311+G(2d,p) level of theory with the geometries obtained from the X-ray structure analyses. Calculated absolute shielding values were converted to relative shifts (δ) by calculating the shielding of tetramethylsilane at the same level of theory.
