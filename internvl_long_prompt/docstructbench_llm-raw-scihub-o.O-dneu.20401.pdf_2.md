The role of BDNF in regulating GABAergic and glycinergic transmission in dorsal horn, however, has not been fully investigated. Furthermore, the distribution of full length (fl) functional trkB receptors in rat and mouse lamina II (Salio et al., 2005), the presence of BDNF in these lamina (Michael et al., 1997), and the inhibitory effect of BDNF on inhibitory interneurons in dorsal horn (Battaglia et al., 2003) have not been reported yet. In this study, we tested the hypothesis that BDNF can control the release of GABA and glycine in superficial dorsal horn. Since local GABAergic and glycinergic inhibition is critical for gating of nociceptive transmission (Malan et al., 1996; Bowery, 1996), any effect of BDNF on inhibitory neurotransmission could have a great impact on pain signalling, especially in pathological conditions of persistent pain. By using the patch-clamp technique, we have studied the modulatory effects of BDNF on spontaneous and evoked release of GABA and glycine in lamina II neurons from neonatal rats. Furthermore, we have determined the pattern of expression of fl-trkB receptors at GABAergic synapses in this lamina.

METHODS

Animals
Experiments were carried out in Sprague-Dawley postnatal rats, ranging in age from 8 (P8) to 30 days (P30). Patch-clamp experiments were carried out in P8-P16 animals, whereas histology was performed in young-adult rats (P25-P30). All experimental procedures were in strict accordance with the guidelines of the European Union and regulation of animal welfare, and previously authorized by the Italian Ministry of Health.

Electrophysiological Recordings

Slice Preparation. Postnatal Sprague-Dawley rats were anesthetized with halothane and decapitated, the spinal cord was rapidly removed and placed in ice-cold Krebs' solution (composition in mM: 125 NaCl, 2.5 KCl, 1.25 NaH2PO4, 26 NaHCO3, 25 glucose, 1 MgCl2 and 2 CaCl2, pH 7.4, bubbled with 95% O2 and 5% CO2). The lumbar block of the spinal cord was isolated, laid down on an agarose block, and transverse slices (500 µm thick) were obtained using a vibrating microtome (Campden Instruments, UK). Slices were incubated in oxygenated Krebs' solution at 35°C for 1 h and used for recording within 6-7 h.

Patch-Clamp Recording and Whole-Cell Configuration
Patch-clamp recordings were performed on visually identified lamina II neurons at room temperature. Neurons were visualized using a Zeiss Axioskop microscope (Dage-MTI, USA), Slides optics were connected to a camera (Dage-MTI, USA), and images were captured at 1-2 frames/s, 3.5- Thick-walled borosilicate pipettes, having a resistance of 2-4 MΩ, were filled with a solution containing (in mM): 130 Cs-glucuronate, 10 CsCl, 11 EGTA, 1 CaCl2, 10 HEPES, and 2 Mg2+-ATP, pH adjusted to 7.2 with NaOH, osmolarity adjusted to 305 mOsm. Junction potential was corrected after recording.

Data were recorded and acquired using a Multiclamp 700A amplifier and pClamp 9 software (Molecular Devices, USA). Sampling rate was 10 kHz, and data were filtered at 2 kHz. Both miniature inhibitory postsynaptic currents (mIPSCs) and evoked inhibitory postsynaptic currents (eIPSCs) were recorded in Krebs' solution, in constant presence of 0.1 µM NBQX and 5 µM TTX; only for the mIPSCs recording, 10 µM TTX was added to the bath solution. Evoked IPSCs were obtained by a focal stimulating the tissue around the Krebs' recorded cell with a second patch-clamp pipette filled with Krebs' solution. The second patch-clamp pipette was repetitively stimulated by a silver wire placed close to the slice. Stimuli had intensities of 0.1-0.5 mA and durations of 1 ms. Evoked IPSCs with constant latency and no failures during 1 Hz stimulation were considered monosynaptic and tested for BDNF.

Data Analysis and Statistics. Miniature IPSC amplitudes, frequencies, and kinetics were determined using Minianalysis software (Synaptosoft, Decatur, USA). Detection threshold for mIPSCs was set at 8-10 pA. Selection of mIPSCs was performed manually and events with rise-time higher than 3 ms were manually discarded (the exclusion of these events did not affect the results of the analysis). Analysis of the results was performed on the same time window (10 min) during control and when the BDNF effect was stabilized.
