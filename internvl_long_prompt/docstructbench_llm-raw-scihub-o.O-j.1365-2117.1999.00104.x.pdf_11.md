### Interpretation
Tir 2 has not been drilled in the Tuscan Shelf, but it has similar character and stratigraphic position to Seq 2. Therefore, it is probably correlative with Lit 2 + Lit 3 of the Volterra Basin. This correlation is supported by the fact that Lit 3 crops out on the Tuscan Shelf at the Secche di Vada Shoal (as _et al._, 1981).

The prograding cliniforms and the mounded reflections (sp. 900-1000; Fig. 19), traces of gently prograding cliniforms can be observed and, in places (T7, sp. 500; Fig. 19), mounded reflections have been recognized. The maximum thickness of Tir 6 is 0.6 s TWT.

Tir 3 has been recognized only in the Ombrone area (lines: T4A, T5B, T6, T7, T8, Figs 13, 20). It is bounded at the base by unconformity B* and at the top by unconformity C (or its correlative conformity). It is characterized by well-defined, discontinuous, closely spaced reflections, normally onlapping onto the basal unconformity. Toward the top, the reflections become more widely spaced and less defined. At the bottom, in several profiles, the reflections are mounded and very well defined (in T7, sp. 400-500, Fig. 19; T8 sp. 1100-1200, Fig. 20).
