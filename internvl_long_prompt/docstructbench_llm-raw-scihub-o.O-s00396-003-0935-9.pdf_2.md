**Polymer synthesis**

A micellar polymerization technique was used to prepare polyelectrolyte poly(acrylamide-acrylic acid-N-(4-butylphenylacrylamide) [P(AM-AA-BPAM)] was synthesized. The rheological properties were investigated in more detail. Pyrene probe fluorescence (APF), dynamic light scattering (DLS), and atomic force microscopy (AFM) were used to explore the conformational behaviors which are dependent on the electrostatic and hydrophobic interactions.

**Experimental**

**Materials and synthesis of monomers and polymers**

AM, 4-butylaniline, was purchased from Fluka Chemical Co. and was used as received. AA was purified by recrystallization from deionized water.

**Monomer synthesis**

BPAM was synthesized from the reaction of 4-butylaniline with acryloyl chloride, triethylamine as the acid receptor, using the method described by McCormick et al. [26]. The crude product was recrystallized twice from ethanol at 92-93 °C. FTIR (KBr): ν = 3353 cm<sup>-1</sup>, 2927 cm<sup>-1</sup>, 1686 cm<sup>-1</sup>, 1605 cm<sup>-1</sup>, 1516 cm<sup>-1</sup>, 1440 cm<sup>-1</sup>, 1302 cm<sup>-1</sup>, 1166 cm<sup>-1</sup>, 1060 cm<sup>-1</sup>, 833 cm<sup>-1</sup>, 752 cm<sup>-1</sup>, 696 cm<sup>-1</sup>.

**Measurements**

Elemental analysis was performed with an Elementar-CHNO-Rapid to determine the carbon, nitrogen, and hydrogen content. The Fourier transform IR measurement was conducted using a Bruker Tensor 27 spectrometer. The DLS and IR measurements were carried out on the BPAM solution in CDCl<sub>3</sub> using a Bruker D8 Advance, UV spectrophotometer (λ<sub>ex</sub> = 335 nm, I = 573 mW, λ<sub>em</sub> = 384 nm), Spectrofluorometer (λ<sub>ex</sub> = 335 nm, I = 573 mm, λ<sub>em</sub> = 384 nm), Viscosimeter (η = 1.73 mm<sup>2</sup>/s) were obtained with a Spectrophoto-meter (λ<sub>ex</sub> = 335 nm, I = 573 mV, λ<sub>em</sub> = 384 nm). The viscosity measurements were conducted with a Bohlin CS rheometer with a double-gap 42 system. The rheological measurements were performed with a cone/plate geometry with a diameter of 40 mm. The zero-shear viscosity is applicable for a 4° angle and a low-shear rate of 0.1 s<sup>-1</sup>. The zero-shear viscosity was measured with a Paar OCR-D oscillating dilute solution was measured with a shear rate of 6 s<sup>-1</sup>. The measurement temperature was 25 ± 0.1 °C, and the atmosphere was nitrogen. The digital thermometer was made using a Digital Instruments NanoScope III controller. The AFM micrographs were made using a 3100 Digital Instruments and Nanoscope III controller. The measurements were performed in tapping mode. Mica wafers were used as the substrate for the measurements. The samples were prepared by dip-coating and drying up. Dynamic light scattering studies were performed at 60-120° and 290-300°A signals were recorded with a Brookhaven Instruments BI-200AT correlator. Effective hydrodynamic diameters were calculated using the algorithm CONTIN and associated software.
