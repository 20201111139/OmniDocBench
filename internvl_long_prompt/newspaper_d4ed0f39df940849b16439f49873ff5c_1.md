Workshop Objectives
The Protected Species Safe Handling, Release, and Identification Workshops are designed to teach the longline and gillnet required to teach the longline and gillnet techniques for the safe handling and release of entangled, such as sea turtles, marine mammals, and smalltooth sawfish. In addition to improve reporting, the proper identification of protected species will also be taught at these workshops. Additionally, individuals attending these workshops will gain a better understanding of the requirements for participating in these fisheries. The overall goal of these workshops is to provide participants with the skills needed to reduce the mortality of protected species, which may prevent additional regulations on these fisheries in the future.

Abstract: The Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act (Dodd-Frank Act) provides the Bureau with supervisory, enforcement, and rulemaking authority over a range of consumer financial products and services. Among other things, the Dodd-Frank Act provides the Bureau with the authority to collect information from companies regarding consumer complaints. The Bureau is developing a complaint intake system, which will allow companies to proactively participate in the Bureau’s Company Portal (Company Portal), a secure, Web-based interface between the Bureau’s systems and companies.

The Bureau’s proposed form, the Company Portal Boarding Form (Boarding Form), will serve as the basis for the interface between the Bureau’s systems and companies. The Boarding Form will collect information from companies, result in a greatly enhanced and efficient experience from both the companies and the Bureau.
