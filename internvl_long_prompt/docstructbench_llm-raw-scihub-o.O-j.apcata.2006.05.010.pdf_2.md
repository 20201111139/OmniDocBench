On a well-balanced catalyst, the hydrocracking activity and selectivity mainly depends on the acid properties of the support. Thus, catalysts based on amorphous silica-alumina having moderate Brønsted acidity are more selective towards zeolites (4,23-25). In the case of zeolitic catalysts, improved selectivity towards middle distillates can be attained by reducing the Brønsted acid sites, that is, by increasing the Si/Al ratio (26,27), or by decreasing their acid strength through partial or total isomorphous substitution of framework Al^3+ by other trivalent cations such as B^3+, Hf^4+ (28). However, the gain in selectivity obtained after replacing the zeolite acid sites is always accompanied by a decrease in the catalyst activity, thus requiring higher reaction temperatures to achieve a given degree of conversion.

Therefore, there is still great interest in developing new catalysts for the hydrocracking-hydroisomerization of high boiling point n-paraffins, such as those produced in the Fischer-Tropsch (F-T) process. A review dealing with suitable catalysts for the hydroisomerization of long-chain n-paraffins has been recently published by Daltari (22).

On the basis of the above discussion, in this work we address the influence of the tungsten coverage on the catalytic behaviour of P/WO3-ZrO2 catalysts for the hydroconversion of long-chain n-alkanes produced in F-T processes. The WO3-ZrO2 samples used here have been prepared by coprecipitation in the presence of polyvinyl alcohol (PVA) leading to samples with enhanced surface areas as compared to conventional coprecipitation and impregnation methods (51). Interestingly, we have found that the tungsten coverage in P/WO3-ZrO2 catalysts has a strong influence on the methods (51). Interestingly, we have found that the tungsten coverage in P/WO3- ZrO2 catalysts has a strong influence on the catalytic performance of P/WO3-ZrO2 based on bifunctional catalysts for the hydroisomerization of short-chain n-alkanes (30-41). The amount of tungsten in heavier n-alkanes would be more representative of the waxy Fischer-Tropsch products is very scarce (29,42-45).

The catalytic properties of P/WO3-ZrO2 materials are largely influenced by the method of preparation, chemical composition, and activation conditions. Then, it is not surprising that different optimum compositions (W loading) are found in the literature depending on the method of preparation and calcination temperature.

2. Experimental

2.1. Preparation of catalysts

A series of tungsten-doped zirconia samples (abbreviated as WZ) with tungsten loading in the range of 6-22 wt. % have been prepared by coprecipitation in polyvinyl alcohol (PVA) following the procedure described in Ref. [53] with some modifications. Chiefly, 300 cm^3 of a 53 mM zirconyl chloride (Aldrich, 98% WZrOCl2-8H2O) and ammonium metatungstate (Fluka, 85% (NH4)6H2W12O40) solutions were heated at 100 °C in an oil bath and kept at this temperature for 1 h. Besides, 300 cm^3 of a polyvinyl alcohol (PVA, MW = 7 x 10^4 - 1 x 10^5, Sigma) aqueous solution (10 g two solutions were mixed and kept at 100 °C for an additional hour. After that, the mixed solution was cooled until solution pH constant at 12 and kept for one additional hour in static. The formed transparent gel filaments were extensively washed with distilled water until the absence of chlorides and dried at 100 °C overnight and at 200 °C for an additional 12 h period.
