## Abstract

A new ionic conjugated polymer, poly(2-ethynyl-N-methylpyridinium iodide) (PEMPI), was synthesized by the direct polymerization of 2-ethynylpyridine and methyl iodide under mild reaction condition. Another similar conjugated polymer with different counter-ion, poly2-ethynyl-N-methylpyridinium tetraphenylborate (PEMPTPB) was also prepared by the ion-exchange reaction of poly(2-ethynyl-N-methylpyridinium iodide) using sodium tetraphenylborate. The polymer structures were characterized by various instrumental methods having conjugated polymer system with pyridyl moieties of different counter-ion. The photoluminescence spectra of these polymers have indicated that there is no different inductive and size effects of counter-anion between iodide and tetraphenylborate anions in this conjugated polymer.

## 1. Introduction

Polycetylene and its homologues have been studied as organic semiconductors [1-3], membranes for gas separation [4], and for liquid-mixture separation [5,6]. Chiro-optical materials [7], chemical sensors [8], side-chain liquid crystals [9], and materials for nonlinear optical property [10,11] and photoluminescence and electro-troluminescence properties [12-17] have potential as materials for mixed ionic and electronic conductivities, energy storage devices such as batteries and permselective membranes, and light-emitting devices due to their extensive conjugation and various functionalities. The synthesis of simple mono- and disubstituted ionic polycetylenes from ethynylpyridines had been carried out with the activation of acetylene bond in ethynylpyridines [18,19]. We also studied on the synthesis and characterization of the poly(2-ethynylpyridine)-based conjugated polymers having various functionalities [2,9,14].

## 2. Experimental

The conjugated ionic polymer, poly(2-ethynyl-N-methylpyridinium iodide) (PMEPI), was poly-prepared by the direct polymerization of 2-ethynylpyridine with methyl iodide without any initiator system in methanol solvent. The polymerization was performed at 70 ℃ after standing for 1 h because of the low boiling temperature of methyl iodide. Another ionic polymer, poly(2-ethynyl-N-methylpyridinium tetraphenylborate) (PEMPTPB) was easily prepared by the ion-exchange reaction of PMEPI with sodium tetraphenylborate in methanol solvent.
