## 4. Conclusions

The addition of high-pressure to PEC instrumentation (now referred to as PPEC) has improved the efficiency of this technique by producing homogeneous voltage flow profiles that are near theoretical predictions. Linear voltage profiles have finally been achieved, as well as reduced plate equilibration times. These improvements are attributed to the increased efficiency of the electroosmotic forces acting on the reduced solvent volume. Also, the use of higher field strengths will increase the utility of this technique. This can be easily implemented with an efficient heat sink to combat Joule heating.
