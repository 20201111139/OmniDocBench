# 3.6. Correlation between catalytic activity and acidity

As mentioned above, the active site responsible for dimerization is suggested to consist of a low valent nickel ion and an acid, as observed in the nickel-containing catalyst (4,17,18). The term "low valent nickel" was originally derived from the fact that the nickel in NiSO₄ is drastically positioned by the carbon monoxide, since a low valent nickel is favorable to chemisorb CO (47,48). Previously, from the results of IR and XPS to identify low valent nickel species, we reported that the active sites responsible for ethylene dimerization consist of a low-valent nickel, Ni⁺ and an acid (41). Also, it was reported that the adsorption site of CO is responsible for the adsorption site of ethylene. Therefore, it is concluded that a low-valent nickel, Ni⁺, plays the role of an adsorption site for ethylene, while the acid site is responsible for the formation of Ni⁺. The adsorption site for ethylene is remarkably higher than that of acid sites for 2NiSO₄/2STiO₂-75ZrO₂, 2NiSO₄/7STiO₂-25ZrO₂, and 2NiSO₄/7STiO₂-25ZrO₂, showing a maximum acidity for 2NiSO₄/5STiO₂-50ZrO₂. We examined the effect of TiO₂-ZrO₂ composition on the catalytic activity for ethylene dimerization. The catalytic activities for several catalysts against reaction time are plotted in Fig. 10. It is clear that the catalytic activities of three NiSO₄ catalysts are supported on binary oxide TiO₂-ZrO₂ (2.6-3.7 mmol/g) remarkably higher than those of single oxide, 2NiSO₄/TiO₂ and 2NiSO₄/ZrO₂. This can be explained if the number of acid sites of TiO₂-ZrO₂-supported catalysts is considerably larger than those of TiO₂ or ZrO₂-supported catalysts, as seen in Table 2. It has been reported that the catalytic activity for ethylene dimerization is closely correlated with the acidity of the catalyst (4,10,35). Therefore, we conclude that the effect of TiO₂-ZrO₂ composition is related to an increase in the number of surface acid sites and to the increased surface area of TiO₂-ZrO₂-supported catalysts. Within supported NiSO₄ intermediates, such as ethyl cations (41), Kazansky et al. concluded that Ni⁺ ions in NiCa-Y zeolite are responsible for the catalytic activity (15). EPR spectroscopy was used to identify low valent nickel species. Therefore, without NiSO₄, the catalytic activity of TiO₂-ZrO₂ is inactive for ethylene dimerization, as shown in Fig. 10.

It is known that for ethylene dimerization the variations in catalytic activities are closely correlated to the acidity values of catalyst (3,35). The acidity values of several samples after evacuation at 350 ℃ are listed in Table 2. The catalytic activities in Table 2 shows that the catalytic activities substantially run parallel to the acidity values. The catalytic activities of 2NiSO₄/TiO₃, 2NiSO₄/ZrO₂ and NiSO₄/TiO₂-ZrO₂ were examined as a function of TiO₂-ZrO₂ composition. The results are shown in Fig. 11, where catalysts were evaluated at 500 ℃ for 1 h before a reaction. It is confirmed that the catalytic activity gives a maximum at 2NiSO₄/5STiO₂-50ZrO₂. This seems to be correlated to the specific surface area and to the acidity of the catalysts. The acidity of catalysts calculated at 500 ℃ was determined by the amount of NH₃ irreversibly adsorbed at 230 ℃ (13,4,9,38). The amount of NH₃ in Fig. 11, respectively, higher the acidity, the higher the catalytic activity. In this way it is demonstrated that the catalytic activity of supported NiSO₄ catalysts essentially run parallel to the acidity. Good correlations have been found in many cases between the acidity and the catalytic activities of solid acids. It has been reported that the catalytic dimerization values as well as nickel-containing catalysts isomerization are closely correlated with the acidity of the catalysts (3,4,6,35). Up to now, a correlation between catalytic activity for ethylene dimerization and acidity has only been studied using ethylene catalytic activity for NiSO₄, (19,35,47), where only one kind of support material such as Al₂O₃, TiO₂-ZrO₂, and Al₂O₃-ZrO₂ was used. However, in this kind of work we used different supports of NiSO₄, ZrO₂, TiO₂-ZrO₂, and TiO₂-ZrO₂, there was also a correlation between catalytic activity for ethylene dimerization and acidity, regardless of the kind of support (TiO₂, ZrO₂, and TiO₂-ZrO₂).
