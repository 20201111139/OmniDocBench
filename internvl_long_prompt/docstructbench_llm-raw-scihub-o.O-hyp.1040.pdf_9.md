The condition of the distribution of the four opportunists may be associated with physical or biological conditions on the glacier surface. The alga of Rapidiophenomena has been found in eastern Europe and in the Antarctic, and one of the species of Rapidiophenomena (Rapidiophenomena kolii) was reported as a shade-adapted species (Komarek et al., 1973). However, the distribution of Rapidiophenomena on this glacier cannot be explained by the shade-light characteristics. The distribution of this alga (mainly at S4 and a little at S1) corresponded to the area where higher species diversity and may be able to survive in areas with keen species competition. The distribution of Oscillatoriacean 1 may be associated with the distribution of cryoconite holes. A cryoconite hole is a water-filled depression on the glacial ice surface, and is inhabited by many organisms, including algae, cyanobacteria, rotifers, and insects (e.g. Wharton et al., 1985). On Gulkana Glacier, cryoconite holes occurred mainly at sites S2 and S3. A larger amount of Oscillatoriacean 1 was observed at the bottom of cryoconite holes relative to the bare-ice surface (not quantified), suggesting that the cryoconite hole is a suitable habitat for this alga. The alga on the ice surface in the area may be derived from cryoconite holes. The conditions for Cry. brebissonii and Oscillatoriacean 2, observed at only S1, were uncertain. Since cyanobacteria typically do not grow well at below pH 5.5, the pH condition of glacier surface may affect the distribution of cyanobacteria.
