The WFCCRDA (Pub. A Mineral Potential Report was completed on June 7, 2013. According to the LCCRDA (Pub. L. 108-424), section 102(g), lands identified within the Ely Resource Management Plan are withdrawn from location and entry under the mining and from operation under the mineral and geothermal leasing laws until such time as the Secretary of the Interior (Secretary) terminates the withdrawal or the lands are patented.

In accordance with the LCCRDA and WFCCRDA, 85 percent of the funds generated by this sale will be used for archaeological resources, natural resource protection, recreation and wilderness planning, and other opportunities in Lincoln and White Pine County respectively. Additionally, five percent of the revenue will go to the State of Nevada General Education Fund and 10 percent of the revenue will go to Imicoln or White Pine County respectively.
