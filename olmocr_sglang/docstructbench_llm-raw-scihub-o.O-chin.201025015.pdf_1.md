Mixed Cations and Structural Complexity in \((\text{Eu}_1 - x, \text{Ca}_x)\text{In}_2\text{Ge}_4\) and
\((\text{Eu}_1 - x, \text{Ca}_x)\text{In}_2\text{Ge}_3\) — The First Two Members of the Homologous Series
\(A_{2[1+1]}\text{In}_{2[1+m]}\text{Ge}_{2[1+n]}\) (\(n, m = 1, 2, \ldots\); \(A\): \text{Ca, Sr, Ba, Eu, or Yb}). —
\(\text{Eu}_{1.25}\text{Ca}_{2.75}\text{In}_3\text{Ge}_4\) (I), \(\text{Eu}_{2.60}\text{Ca}_{1.40}\text{In}_3\text{Ge}_4\) (II), \(\text{Eu}_{0.30}\text{Ca}_{2.70}\text{In}_2\text{Ge}_3\) (III), and
\(\text{Eu}_{0.60}\text{Ca}_{2.34}\text{In}_2\text{Ge}_3\) (IV) are synthesized from the elements in a molten In metal flux
(960 °C, 20 h). The compounds are characterized by single crystal XRD, magnetic sus-
ceptibility measurements, and TB-LMTO band structure calculations. (I) and (II) crys-
tallize in the space group \(\text{C2}/\text{m}\) with \(Z = 2\), and (III) and (IV) in the space group \(\text{Pnma}\)
with \(Z = 4\). The polyanionic substructures of the compounds are based on \(\text{InGe}_4\) edge-
sharing tetrahedra, \(\text{Ge}_2\) dimers, and bridging In atoms in a nearly square planar envi-
ronment. — (YOU, T.-S.; TOBASH, P. H.; BOBEV*, S.; Inorg. Chem. 49 (2010) 4,
1773-1783; Dep. Chem. Biochem., Univ. Del., Newark, DE 19716, USA; Eng.) —
