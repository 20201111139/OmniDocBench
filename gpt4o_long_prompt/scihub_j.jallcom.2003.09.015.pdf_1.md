The formation of solid solution regions for the binary compounds was not possible, and special attention requires the attempts to prepare ternary borides known for the isogonal systems.  
A new compound of the composition ErNi<sub>3</sub>B was reported to form LnNi<sub>10</sub>B where Ln=Y, Gd, Tb, Dy, Ho with the Th<sub>2</sub>Ni<sub>10</sub>B structure type were reported in [12]. We prepared the sample of the composition Er<sub>2</sub>NiBi<sub>10</sub> to verify the formation of the bordic with the same structure in the Er–Ni–B system. But the powder diffraction pattern of this sample contained the lines of three binary compounds (ErB<sub>4</sub> + ErB<sub>2</sub> + NiB), which indicates the absence of the ternary boride Er<sub>2</sub>NiBi<sub>10</sub> in the Er–Ni–B system (Table 2).

Thus, an example of the existence of polymorphism for this boride. Taking into account that the boride with the ErNiB<sub>4</sub> type structure was grown by a single-crystal method, and that the boride with the YCrB<sub>4</sub> type structure was found by X-ray powder diffraction, we assumed the high-temperature modification to have the ErNiB<sub>4</sub> structure and the low-temperature modification to have the YCrB<sub>4</sub> structure. The results of a DTA investigation revealed a transition temperature of 1450K. 

A new compound of the approximate composition ErNi<sub>3</sub>B<sub>3</sub> with unknown structure was found. The X-ray powder pattern of this compound differed from the powder patterns of the known ternary borides found in the earlier studied Ln–Ni–B systems. This compound exists only in the as-cast sample and decomposes into the Er<sub>2</sub>Ni<sub>12</sub>B<sub>6</sub> and ErNi<sub>3</sub>B compounds after annealing. Since the single crystals were not available its structure was not determined.

A further new compound was found in the as-cast samples containing 16.6 at.% Er. The X-ray powder pattern of this compound was very similar to the powder pattern of the ErNiB<sub>4</sub> boride. The crystal structure of the new Er<sub>0.917</sub>Ni<sub>4.098</sub>B boride was determined using an X-ray single-crystal method: space group P6/mmc; \( a = 1.48399 \, \mathrm{nm} \); \( c = 0.69194 \, \mathrm{nm} \). This structure is very similar to the Y<sub>0.915</sub>Ni<sub>4.12</sub>B structure [16]. The Er<sub>0.917</sub>Ni<sub>4.098</sub>B boride decomposes after annealing, and the main phase is the ErNi<sub>3</sub>B compound.
