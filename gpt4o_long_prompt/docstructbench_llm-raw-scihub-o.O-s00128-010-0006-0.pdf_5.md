The dominance of ionic species in precipitation areas is represented in Table 8. In Alibagh \(\text{Cl} > \text{Na} > \text{Mg} > \text{Ca} > \text{SO}_4 > \text{NO}_3 > \text{NH}_4 > \text{K}\), Goa \(\text{Cl} > \text{Na} > \text{Ca} > \text{Mg} > \text{SO}_4 > \text{NO}_3 > \text{K}\), and Bombay \(\text{Cl} > \text{Na} > \text{Ca} > \text{Mg} > \text{SO}_4 > \text{NO}_3 > \text{K}\) showed dominance of Cl, whereas at Colaba \(\text{Na} > \text{Cl} > \text{Ca} > \text{Mg} > \text{SO}_4 > \text{NO}_3 > \text{NH}_4 > \text{K}\), Silent Valley \(\text{Na} > \text{Ca} > \text{Cl} > \text{NO}_3 > \text{SO}_4 > \text{Mg} > \text{K}>\text{NH}_4\), Chembur \(\text{Na} > \text{Cl} > \text{Ca} > \text{NO}_3 > \text{NH}_4 > \text{Mg} > \text{SO}_4 > \text{K}\), there is a dominance of Na as these sites are nearer to sea coast (Naik et al. 2002; Rao et al. 1995; Khemani et al. 1994).
