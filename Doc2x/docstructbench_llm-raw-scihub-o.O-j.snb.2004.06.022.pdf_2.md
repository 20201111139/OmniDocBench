The main goal of this paper is to investigate the electric resistance of a layered phthalocyanine and palladium (Pc \( + \mathrm{{Pd}} \) ) structure under the influence of various hydrogen concentrations in nitrogen and air. We show that during the interaction of hydrogen with bilayer structures metal-free phthalocyanine and palladium there occurs a strong change in the resistance of these structures. This would correspond to an acousto-electric effect in the SAW sensor system
